What is all this?

This is the Automatic MIDI Inverter! Inspired by Andrew Huang, the basic idea is to start from the first note and pivot every note from above to below, and vice versa, while keeping the same interval. For a more detailed explanation and example, watch Andrew's video about it.

I provide two options for how the algorithm inverts the notes. The first option is Andrew's original idea, where the first note stays the same and all the notes are flipped around that note. This can result in inversions with much higher or lower pitch ranges than their originals, depending on where the first note is within the range. The second option finds the pitch in the center of the range and flips everything around that pitch, which keeps the range of the inversion the same as that of the original.
